Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

This traditional Roman pasta dish is quick, simple, and delicious. Made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper, Spaghetti Carbonara brings an authentic taste of Italy to your table. No cream needed â€” just pure, creamy goodness from the ingredients themselves!

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large pan over medium heat, cook the pancetta until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot pasta to the pan with pancetta and toss to coat.
  6. Remove from heat and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing vigorously to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time if needed to loosen the sauce.
  7. Serve immediately, topped with extra Pecorino and black pepper.
